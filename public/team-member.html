General Terms & Conditions

Please read these terms and conditions carefully before booking any travel services with Treker Tours and Travels. By booking with us, you agree to be bound by these conditions.

1. Financial Responsibilities

1.1 Payment Policies
  • All tour packages require a non-refundable deposit of 25% at the time of booking.
  • Full payment is required 30 days prior to the departure date.
  • For bookings made within 30 days of departure, full payment is required at the time of booking.
  • Payments can be made via credit card, bank transfer, or other methods specified during the booking process.
  • All prices are quoted in USD unless otherwise specified.
1.2 Cancellation and Refund Policy
  • Cancellations made 60 days or more before departure: Full refund minus the deposit.
  • Cancellations made 30-59 days before departure: 50% refund minus the deposit.
  • Cancellations made less than 30 days before departure: No refund will be provided.
  • All cancellation requests must be submitted in writing.
  • Refunds will be processed within 14 business days of approval.
1.3 Travel Insurance
  • Travel insurance is highly recommended for all travelers.
  • Treker does not provide travel insurance and is not responsible for any losses or expenses due to trip cancellation, medical emergencies, or other unforeseen circumstances.
  • Customers are advised to purchase comprehensive travel insurance covering trip cancellation, medical expenses, evacuation, and personal belongings.

2. Baggage and Personal Items

2.1 What to Carry
  • Valid passport with at least six months validity beyond your return date.
  • Appropriate visa documentation for your destination.
  • Personal medications and prescriptions in original packaging.
  • Travel insurance documentation.
  • Credit/debit cards and a reasonable amount of cash for emergencies.
  • Photocopies of important documents (passport, visa, insurance) stored separately from originals.
  • Weather-appropriate clothing and footwear.
2.2 Baggage Allowance
  • Baggage allowances vary depending on the mode of transportation and specific tour package.
  • For most tours, travelers are permitted one checked bag (maximum 23kg/50lbs) and one carry-on bag.
  • Additional baggage may incur extra charges as per carrier policies.
  • Treker is not responsible for any baggage fees imposed by airlines or other transportation providers.
2.3 Prohibited Items
  • Illegal substances and drugs (unless prescribed by a doctor).
  • Weapons, including firearms, knives, and self-defense items.
  • Flammable or hazardous materials.
  • Plants, seeds, or agricultural products (restrictions vary by destination).
  • Counterfeit goods or items prohibited by destination countries.
  • Large amounts of cash (check destination country regulations).
  • Certain electronic devices may be restricted in some destinations.
2.4 Valuables and Liability
  • Treker is not responsible for any loss, theft, or damage to personal belongings during the tour.
  • Valuables such as jewelry, expensive electronics, and large sums of money should be left at home.
  • If you must travel with valuables, they should be kept with you at all times or stored in hotel safes when available.
  • We recommend purchasing travel insurance that covers loss or theft of personal items.

3. Tour Modifications and Disruptions

3.1 Tour Changes
  • Treker reserves the right to alter itineraries, accommodations, or included services if necessary due to weather, safety concerns, or other circumstances beyond our control.
  • In the event of significant changes, we will notify customers as soon as possible and offer alternatives where available.
  • No refunds will be provided for minor deviations from the itinerary.
3.2 Force Majeure
  • Treker is not liable for any delays, cancellations, or changes due to events beyond our control, including but not limited to: natural disasters, political unrest, terrorism, strikes, epidemics, or governmental regulations.
  • In such cases, we will attempt to provide alternate arrangements where possible but cannot guarantee refunds for services not utilized.

4. Customer Responsibilities

4.1 Documentation
  • It is the customer's responsibility to ensure they have all necessary travel documents, including passports, visas, and health certificates.
  • Treker is not responsible for denied entry or exit due to improper documentation.
4.2 Health and Fitness
  • Customers must inform Treker of any pre-existing medical conditions that may affect their participation in tour activities.
  • Travelers are responsible for assessing whether a tour is suitable for their physical capabilities.
  • Treker reserves the right to decline service to anyone whose health or behavior may impede the operation of a tour or negatively affect other participants.
4.3 Conduct
  • Customers must comply with local laws, customs, and regulations of all visited destinations.
  • Customers are expected to respect cultural sensitivities and follow guidance from tour leaders.
  • Treker reserves the right to terminate participation of any customer whose behavior is deemed inappropriate, with no refund provided.
